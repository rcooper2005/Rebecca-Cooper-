Background

About Dr. Cooper

Bridging Science, Education & Technology

Dr. Cooper is a professor with backgrounds in biology, environmental science, and science education, bringing 22 years of higher education experience. Her leadership includes eight years as Chair of Assessment and Graduate Department Chair, and she currently serves as the Director of Assessment at Georgia Gwinnett College.

Dr. Cooper has wide-ranging experience teaching science content, methods, and general education courses to undergraduate and graduate students, notably developing and teaching online courses for over 18 years. Beyond her instruction and supervision of field students, she provides support and training to faculty on integrating technology and online instruction.

Dr. Cooper is an emerging leader in AI education research, investigating how artificial intelligence is transforming teacher preparation. Her research on pre-service teachers' perceptions of AI has gained international recognition, most recently through her presentation at the eLearn 2025 World Conference on EdTech, where she shared findings on pre-service educators' evolving perspectives on AI for professional development and learning.

Her AI scholarship includes publications in Gateways examining whether pre-service teachers view AI as friend or foe, and in the Journal of Advances in Education exploring the evolution of preparing preservice teachers for the new digital classroom. To inform her research and practice, Dr. Cooper has earned multiple AI certifications, including Magic School AI Certified Educator (Levels 1-3), Magic School AI Image Generator, Writing.io Academy AI Course, and Khanmigo for Educators.
